About the Chart

In a hypothetical three-way race between Bernie Sanders, Hillary Clinton and Donal Trump, what percentage of Democrats, Republicans and independents would vote for each candidate? What would be the outcome?

I promised myself I wouldn't get into prediction in this Black Swan election. But I was curious what a three-way race would look like, how the #bernieorbust movement might impact it, and how other developments were affecting it.

I could not find any really great data. Apparently, no one has done the polling, partly because Sanders has repeatedly said he would not run as an independent if he failed to win the Democratic primary. (If you find some, please send it my way.) However, there is a growing movement that wants Sanders to run as an independent. Others hope Sanders will be the Green Party's candidate. Even if you don't like those ideas, it might be good to think of Bernie as representing the constituency of liberal-minded people who resonate with his anti-establishment message. Where are they going to turn in the future for political representation?

I don't have a crystal ball, but this dynamic chart allows you to put in your own best estimation of which way the political winds are blowing. I hope better polling data will soon become available.

However, there are some polls that suggest certain inclinations.

  1. According to a Wall Street Journal/NBC News poll, 33% of Sanders' supporters cannot see themselves voting for Hillary Clinton in November.
  2. An April 25th Suffolk University poll has found that 19% of Republicans say they will support the Democratic nominee if Donald Trump wins the Republican nomination.
  3. Currently, Sanders is polling at 44% in national primary polls. In national polls against Trump he wins 51% to 38%. (See RealClearPolitics for the latest polls.)
  4. Among independents, Sanders has a huge lead over Clinton, 45% to 29% in this Ipsos/Reuters poll.

There are some assumptions built into this chart that should be considered.

  • We assume that the percentagle of Democrats, Republicans and independents that decide to not vote at all is the same for all groups. That is not likely true, and is highly dependent on multiple factors, but at this point there is not enough data available.
  • This is for the popular vote only, it doesn't take into account the allocation of electors by state.

The percentage of Democrats, Republicans and independents in the electorate is taken from this Gallup Poll. The independents are rounded up 1% to make an even 100% and include third parties like the Green Party and Libertarian Party.

Notes

  1. You can adjust the slider handles to zero, to simulate a two-way race between any two of the candidates.
  2. You can simulate a two-way race between Clinton and Trump and set the Bernie percentages to correspond to the #bernieorbust, write-in and "stay home" numbers.
