About the Chart

In a hypothetical three-way race between Bernie Sanders, Hillary Clinton and Donal Trump, what percentage of Democrats, Republicans and independents would vote for each candidate? What would be the outcome?

I promised myself I wouldn't get into predication in this election. It's a Black Swan election. But I was curious what a three-way race would look like, how the #bernieorbust movement might impact it, and how other developments were affecting it.

I could not find any really great data. Apparently, no one has done the polling, partly because Sanders has repeatedly said he would not run as an independent if he failed to win the Democratic primary. If new data becomes available, it is easy for you to plug it in.

However, there are some numbers you can plug in that produce interesting results.

  1. According to a Wall Street Journal/NBC News poll, 33% of Sanders' supporters cannot see themselves voting for Hillary Clinton in November.
  2. An April 25th Suffolk University poll has found that 19% of Republicans say they will support the Democratic nominee if Donald Trump wins the Republican nomination.
  3. Currently, Sanders is polling at 44% in national primary polls.
  4. Among independents, Sanders has a huge lead over Clinton, 45% to 29% in this Ipsos/Reuters poll.

There are some assumptions built into this chart that should be considered.

  • We assume that the percentagle of Democrats, Republicans and independents that decide to not vote at all is the same for all groups. That is not likely true, and is highly dependent on multiple factors, but at this point there is not enought data available.

The percentage of Democrats, Republicans and independents in the electorate is taken from this Gallup Poll. The independents are rounded up 1% to make an even 100% and include third parties like the Green Party and Libertarian party.
